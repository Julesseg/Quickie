# Living backdrop, hint rotation, and a haptic feedback budget

Supersedes ADR 0010's rejection of an expressive backdrop, and extends its budget discipline to two new expression channels. ADR 0010 rejected an animated mesh backdrop for v1 "in favor of calm and speed"; revisiting that trade-off deliberately, we resolved it by **where** rather than **whether**: the app may be alive at rest, but stays calm in use.

- **Living backdrop.** The quiet two-stop backdrop becomes a subtle purple mesh gradient that drifts very slowly (~20â€“30s period) **only on [[Home]]** and freezes at its current pose the instant a query exists â€” results are always read over a still backdrop, preserving 0010's protection of the typeâ†’chooseâ†’run loop. It renders statically under Reduce Motion, Low Power Mode, and UI test. Always-drifting and imperceptibly-slow variants were rejected: the first puts motion under text mid-loop, the second spends battery on motion no seconds-long launcher session would ever see.
- **Hint rotation.** Home's [[Hint line]] crossfades between hints (~7s cadence) as a new enumerated `MotionMoment` in Core's `MotionPolicy` â€” not a hand-rolled view timer â€” so the motion budget remains the single source of truth. Same degradations: static under Reduce Motion and UI test.
- **Hero shimmer.** The [[Highlighted result]]'s gold hero treatment (ADR 0033, #177) marks `results[0]` with a soft gold gradient glow that **slides gently side to side while a query is being typed and settles to centre about a second after the last keystroke** (debounced, so a burst of keystrokes keeps it moving and only the final pause lands it). This reads the budget the *other way round* from the backdrop: rather than motion at rest that stills in use, it is motion tied to the act of typing that ends when typing ends â€” so a *settled* result is still read over a still glow, while the live, changing highlight feels alive. It stays within the budget's spirit: confined to a single row (the one Enter runs), never moving layout (only the light drifts within the row), and gentle. Earlier tries were dropped â€” a one-shot-on-appear sweep was effectively invisible (it fired during the row's insertion transition and never again), and a circling gold *border* never reliably drew over the row's Liquid Glass. Same degradations: under Reduce Motion and UI test the glow is simply static and centred, no swing and no timer.
- **Feedback budget.** Haptics get the same treatment 0010 gave motion: a `FeedbackPolicy` in `QuickieCore` enumerates the only moments that buzz â€” light impact on running any action (tap or Enter), impact on pin/unpin, selection tick on committing a breadcrumb step, success/error on capture confirmation. Deliberately **no** tick when the [[Highlighted result]] changes: `results[0]` changes on nearly every keystroke, and a per-keystroke buzz under the typing thumb fights the keyboard's own haptics.

The animation feel cannot be verified in cloud sessions (no simulator); tuning constants live in Core where their arithmetic is testable, and CI's XCUITest job gates the frozen-under-test behavior.
